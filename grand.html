Video Game Facts

Grand Theft Auto

Behind the Scenes

  • Real-World Origins: The name "Grand Theft Auto" is derived from an actual police and legal term used to describe the crime of motor vehicle theft.
  • The Infamous Moon: In GTA III and GTA San Andreas, players can shoot the moon with a sniper rifle to cycle through three different sizes. A former developer revealed this was kept in the game because artists couldn't agree on its canonical size during development.
  • Player-Inspired Protagonists: According to IGN, the three protagonists in GTA V represent three distinct types of video game players. Michael is the retired player who wants to take it easy, Franklin represents the ambitious newcomer, and Trevor symbolizes the chaotic player who enjoys the game's sandbox freedom.
  • Missing Planes: Airplanes were notably cut from GTA IV because Rockstar deemed it too soon and inappropriate after the 9/11 tragedy.

Mind-Blowing In-Game Details

  • Simulated Cycles: The moon in GTA V actually follows real-world lunar phases.
  • Dynamic Sunsets: If you switch characters and watch the sunset, each character (Michael, Franklin, or Trevor) will see it in a completely different way.
  • Washing Your Car: If you smash all of your car's windows, drive it through a car wash, and step out, your character's clothes will actually be wet from the water that sprayed inside.
  • The Secret Beating Heart: One of gaming's creepiest mysteries is located inside the Statue of Happiness in GTA IV. If players glitch through a false wall, they will find a massive, beating heart suspended by chains inside the statue.

Easter Eggs and References

  • San Andreas Cameo: During the GTA V mission Hood Safari, you can spot exact, cycling lookalikes of CJ, Big Smoke, and Ryder from GTA San Andreas.
  • No Country for Old Men: The scene in the Chiliad State Wilderness with the burning truck, dead bodies, and a briefcase of cash is a direct, shot-for-shot parody of a famous scene from the movie No Country for Old Men.
